Soil & Agriculture

Sustainable farming practices and soil management techniques

üîÑ Crop Rotation

Benefits:

  • Breaks pest and disease cycles
  • Improves soil structure
  • Balances nutrient demands
  • Reduces soil erosion

Example Rotation:

  • Year 1: Legumes (nitrogen fixation)
  • Year 2: Leafy greens (nitrogen users)
  • Year 3: Root vegetables
  • Year 4: Fruiting crops

üåæ Cover Crops

Types & Benefits:

  • Legumes (clover, vetch) - Fix nitrogen
  • Grasses (rye, oats) - Prevent erosion
  • Brassicas (radish) - Break compaction
  • Buckwheat - Suppress weeds

Functions:

  • Add organic matter
  • Improve soil structure
  • Reduce erosion
  • Suppress weeds

üöú Tillage Practices

Conventional Tillage:

  • Deep plowing and turning
  • Quick seedbed preparation
  • Can cause erosion and compaction

Conservation Tillage:

  • Minimal soil disturbance
  • Leaves crop residue
  • Reduces erosion by 50-90%

No-Till Farming:

  • Zero soil disturbance
  • Maximum residue cover
  • Improves soil health long-term

üåø Organic Amendments

Compost:

  • Improves structure and fertility
  • Apply 1-3 inches annually
  • Increases water retention

Manure:

  • High in nutrients
  • Must be aged/composted
  • Apply in fall for spring planting

Green Manure:

  • Cover crops tilled into soil
  • Adds nitrogen and organic matter

üíß Irrigation Management

Methods:

  • Drip irrigation - 90% efficiency
  • Sprinkler systems - 75% efficiency
  • Furrow irrigation - 60% efficiency

Best Practices:

  • Water deeply but infrequently
  • Irrigate early morning
  • Monitor soil moisture
  • Use mulch to reduce evaporation
  • Match water to crop needs

üß™ Fertilization Strategies

Macronutrients (NPK):

  • Nitrogen - Leaf growth
  • Phosphorus - Root development
  • Potassium - Overall health

Application Methods:

  • Broadcast - Even distribution
  • Banding - Near seed/roots
  • Foliar - Quick absorption
  • Fertigation - Through irrigation

Timing: Based on soil tests and crop needs
