Soil Types & Classification

Discover the diverse world of soil types and their unique characteristics

Sandy Soil

Characteristics:

  • Large particles, good drainage
  • Low water retention
  • Easy to work with
  • Warms up quickly in spring
  • Low nutrient retention

Best for: Root vegetables, herbs, Mediterranean plants

Clay Soil

Characteristics:

  • Fine particles, poor drainage
  • High water retention
  • Rich in nutrients
  • Slow to warm up
  • Can become waterlogged

Best for: Fruit trees, ornamental plants, vegetables with proper drainage

Loam Soil

Characteristics:

  • Balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay
  • Excellent drainage and retention
  • Rich in organic matter
  • Easy to work with
  • Ideal pH levels

Best for: Most crops, vegetables, flowers, lawns

Silt Soil

Characteristics:

  • Medium-sized particles
  • Good water retention
  • Fertile and nutrient-rich
  • Can compact easily
  • Smooth, flour-like texture

Best for: Grasses, bamboo, wetland plants

Peat Soil

Characteristics:

  • High organic matter content
  • Acidic pH (3.5-5.5)
  • Excellent water retention
  • Spongy texture
  • Slow decomposition

Best for: Acid-loving plants, blueberries, azaleas

Chalky Soil

Characteristics:

  • High calcium carbonate content
  • Alkaline pH (7.1-8.5)
  • Free-draining
  • Can be shallow over bedrock
  • Often stony

Best for: Alkaline-loving plants, lilacs, clematis

Soil Classification Systems

USDA Soil Taxonomy

12 major soil orders based on diagnostic horizons and properties:

  • Alfisols - Forest soils with clay accumulation
  • Mollisols - Prairie soils with thick, dark surface
  • Spodosols - Acidic forest soils
  • Ultisols - Highly weathered, acidic soils

World Reference Base (WRB)

International system with 32 reference soil groups:

  • Chernozems - Black earth soils
  • Podzols - Acidic forest soils
  • Ferralsols - Highly weathered tropical soils
  • Vertisols - Swelling clay soils
