ü™® Mineral Components

Primary Minerals:

  • Quartz - Most common, resistant to weathering
  • Feldspar - Source of potassium and aluminum
  • Mica - Provides potassium and magnesium
  • Hornblende - Contains iron and magnesium

Secondary Minerals:

  • Clay minerals (kaolinite, montmorillonite)
  • Iron oxides (hematite, goethite)
  • Aluminum oxides

üåø Organic Matter

Components:

  • Living organisms (roots, microbes, fauna)
  • Fresh organic residues
  • Partially decomposed matter
  • Humus (stable organic matter)

Functions:

  • Nutrient storage and release
  • Soil structure improvement
  • Water retention
  • Microbial habitat

‚öóÔ∏è Chemical Properties

Key Elements:

  • Nitrogen (N) - Protein synthesis
  • Phosphorus (P) - Energy transfer
  • Potassium (K) - Water regulation
  • Calcium (Ca) - Cell wall structure
  • Magnesium (Mg) - Chlorophyll center
  • Sulfur (S) - Amino acid formation

pH Levels:

  • Acidic: 3.5-6.5 (most nutrients available)
  • Neutral: 6.5-7.5 (optimal for most plants)
  • Alkaline: 7.5-9.0 (some nutrients limited)

üèóÔ∏è Physical Structure

Particle Sizes:

  • Clay: < 0.002 mm
  • Silt: 0.002-0.05 mm
  • Sand: 0.05-2.0 mm
  • Gravel: > 2.0 mm

Soil Aggregation:

  • Granular structure (ideal for cultivation)
  • Blocky structure (common in subsoils)
  • Platy structure (restricts water movement)
  • Prismatic structure (found in clay soils)

ü¶† Biological Activity

Soil Organisms:

  • Bacteria - Nutrient cycling, nitrogen fixation
  • Fungi - Decomposition, mycorrhizal associations
  • Protozoa - Nutrient release from bacteria
  • Nematodes - Pest control, nutrient cycling
  • Arthropods - Organic matter breakdown
  • Earthworms - Soil mixing, structure improvement

üíß Water & Air Relations

Water Types in Soil:

  • Gravitational water - Drains freely
  • Capillary water - Available to plants
  • Hygroscopic water - Tightly bound

Porosity:

  • Macropores - Air movement, drainage
  • Micropores - Water storage
  • Total porosity - 40-60% in healthy soils

Soil Horizons

O Horizon

Organic layer with fresh and decomposed plant material

A Horizon

Topsoil with mixed organic matter and minerals

E Horizon

Eluviation layer where materials are leached out

B Horizon

Subsoil with accumulated minerals and clay

C Horizon

Weathered parent material with little biological activity

R Horizon

Bedrock - unweathered parent material
